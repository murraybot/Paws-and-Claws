Cat Care

Responsible cat care involves meeting both their physical and emotional needs. 🐾
Feeding
Proper nutrition is crucial for your cat's health. Choose high-quality, brand-name kitten or cat food based on your cat's age, activity level, and overall health. Ensure that the food is balanced for your cat's life stage and contains essential amino acids like taurine for heart and eye health. Fresh, clean water should always be available, and water bowls should be washed and refilled daily. Remember to limit treats to no more than 5-10% of their diet.
Grooming
Cats are generally fastidious groomers and rarely need baths. However, regular brushing or combing helps maintain their coat. Frequent brushing reduces shedding and minimizes hairballs. If you're unsure about grooming techniques, check out the Cat Grooming Tips from the ASPCA for more information.
Handling
When picking up your cat, place one hand behind the front legs and another under the hindquarters. Lift gently to avoid discomfort. Never pick up a cat by the scruff of the neck or front legs, as this can cause stress or injury.
Housing
Provide your cat with a clean, dry place to sleep and rest. Line their bed with a soft, warm blanket or towel, washing it regularly. Keeping your cat indoors ensures their safety and longevity. Outdoor cats face risks from traffic, fights with other animals, and exposure to infectious diseases.
Identification
If your cat goes outdoors, use a safety collar with an elastic panel and an ID tag. Microchipping your cat provides added security and increases the chances of reuniting with them if they get lost.
Litter Box
All indoor cats need a litter box. Place it in a quiet, accessible location. Regularly scoop out waste and change the litter to maintain cleanliness and prevent odor.
